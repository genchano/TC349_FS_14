Mark Genchanok's Web Design Analysis

Positive Examples:

  • www.google.com

    • Google is a great example of Hick's Law. The decision making is very simple due to limited options that don't confuse users.

  • www.reddit.com

    • Reddit uses proper chunking of information. There are short, descriptive headlines that look organized and structured.

  • www.facebook.com

    • Everyone familiar with Facebook knows it has good mapping. You can scroll down an information feed and still have the main navigation at the top.

  • www.yahoo.com

    • Yahoo has good affordance. No buttons or links are hidden in content or difficult to locate.

  • www.cswd.siarto.com

    • Jeff's website has a great signal-to-noise ratio. The content is all relevant and there are no distracting advertisements.

Negative Examples:

  • www.healthcare.gov

    • This website is bad because of the mapping. There is navigation on the top and more content awkwardly lower on the page.

  • www.reforms.net

    • This webpage takes Hick's Law to the extreme by providing almost no content and no decisions a user can make.

  • www.bolenreport.com

    • The website demonstrates a failure of chunking. Information is all over the page; no one is going to take the time to read it all.

  • www.goetzskyvu.com

    • A poorly designed webpage like this has content poorly placed on backgorund images, making it difficult to read. This is an error with affordance.

  • www.weather.com

    • This website has an absolutely terrible sinal-to-noise ratio. It is full of advertisements and irrelevant news headlines.
